What is Osteoporosis?

    What is Osteoporosis? First, it should be noted that typically, Osteoporosis starts with an age over fifty, when the body cannot produce new tissues and new bones. Hip fractures are among the most common results of Osteoporosis. Hip fractures happen when the front part of the bone, called the femur, is displaced forward by the force of gravity. This often happens because of bad posture or in activities like running or sports, which places excessive stress on the front leg. Osteoporotic fractures are also related to aging


Osteoporotic fractures of the spine occur when the vertebrae shift forward, affecting one or more vertebrae. An accident or certain diseases can cause this shift.

    Osteoporosis also affects younger people, and it is referred to by the term "Osteoporotic Idiopathic." The cause of this kind of osteoporotic fracture is unknown. However, it is common among younger people who engage in weight lifting, bodybuilding, or sports where repeated sudden or dynamic impact to the bones can cause them to break. However, it is commonly observed that Osteoporotic fractures of the spinal column occur in older people whose bones have lost their ability to retain mineral and fluid content that keeps the bones healthy. An osteoporotic fracture occurs when a part of the bone known as the osteoid cartilage is fractured. The cartilage is known to play a vital role in keeping the bones anchored to the floor.
